Deep Dive: Second-Day Play (DNA 13, 14)

This is the biggest gap in V9's current setup registry. SMB DNA 13 lays out a clean framework:

Classic pattern: Strong trend on Day 1 → Day 2 open, look for continuation. Use afternoon support/resistance from Day 1 as your levels. Range typically narrows ~50% on Day 2. Risk ~15% of ATR.

Follow-up pattern (DNA 9): Earnings Day 1 might not trend. Set alerts at key levels for Day 2/3. Institutions buy/sell over 3+ days. AMAT example: inflection at 53 on Day 1, long above 53 on Day 2 → 10:1 R:R by Day 3.

V9 action: Add "SecondDay" to SETUP_REGISTRY with validGP ['A','B','C-Recovery'], nature 'structure', sessions ['OPEN','CORE']. Entry trigger: Day 1 afternoon S/R levels revisited on Day 2 open. Scanner flags needed: track whether a ticker was "in play" yesterday.

Deep Dive: Feed the Downgrade (DNA 14)

Context-dependent: Only fade analyst downgrades when (a) stock already sold off significantly (15-20% from highs), (b) no fresh negative catalyst preceded the downgrade, (c) higher timeframe trend is still intact. Strong stocks + stale downgrade = buy S2 aggressively.

Spencer's heuristic: If it flushes to S2 on the open → aggressive bid. Once back above S1 → add. Trail to R1/R2. Day 2: buy the accumulation zone from Day 1 afternoon.

V9 action: This is a sub-type of Bounce-Reversal (already planned in your integration spec). Wire it as a variant with additional news-catalyst filter: "downgrade/upgrade WITHOUT preceding fundamental catalyst = fade candidate."

Tape Patterns from DNA (Codifiable for V9)

"Fights" on the tape: When you see buying/selling at the same price repeatedly (88, 87, 88, 87) and the stock resolves through that level → that's a signal to add risk in the direction of the break. V9 could track this as "volume cluster at price" in the scanner.

Inability to push through a level: If green prints (offer lifts) keep stopping at the same price (e.g., can't get above 21.40), that resistance is real. Use it as the risk-off level. V9's OR High / VWAP levels already serve this function — the tape reading adds confidence to the level.

"Change in character" = exit signal: DNA 1(5): "150K share volume bar — haven't seen one that big since the open. That's a change in character + pushed above 20. When it pulls back, that's my cover." This maps to your "Reasons to Sell" framework — unusual volume reversal = defensive posture.

Staircase pattern = institutional order: Clean, grinding moves without retesting VWAP = big seller/buyer in the system. "Add risk, add risk, add risk" because this staircase will continue. V9 could detect this as "monotonic price trend + steady volume = trend quality score."

Price Action from DNA 1(4), 4, 5(2)

Move-to-Move vs. Trade-to-Hold

This distinction is critical and currently missing from V9. SMB says: before judging whether you exited well, you must know which type of trade you were in. V9's setup registry could tag each setup with a hold type:

Move-to-Move: Scalps, momentum captures, OR-Break initial push. Target: 1R. Don't regret it going further.

Trade-to-Hold: VWAP-Hold trend rides, Second-Day continuation, BigDog breakout. Target: trail with EMA9/trend line. Hold until "Reason to Sell" fires.

Action: Build a "Reasons to Sell" checklist into the trade management module. Tag each setup type as Move-to-Move or Trade-to-Hold. Add a time-clock field to the pre-trade form: "I expect this to work within ___ minutes." If it doesn't, that's a reason to reduce.

Spencer's Pre-Market Futures Heuristics

Key times: London Open (4:15 AM ET), 8:00 AM ET (institutional orders begin), 9:30 AM (open). Each can establish an overnight high/low that becomes a tradeable level. V9's UMU already notes pre-market levels, but explicitly tracking London Open and 8AM prices as dashboard reference levels would add precision.

When to care about futures: In low-vol uptrending environments — mostly ignore futures, focus on stocks in play. In volatile environments — futures pre-market levels become the most important levels of the day. V9's VIX regime logic could trigger "futures focus mode" when VIX > 20.

Spencer's Macro Catalyst Framework (DNA 15)

The timing of the catalyst matters more than the quality. Negative catalyst after a big run-up → gives people an excuse to sell → follow through to downside. Positive catalyst after a multi-day selloff → gives people an excuse to buy → follow through to upside. "The China tariff thing could be viewed very negatively. Initially we rallied back up... Eventually over the next 4-5 days came down to 280. Then a minor positive catalyst → right back to 289."

V9 translation: Your breadth trajectory already captures this. When breadth has been falling for 3-5 days and a positive catalyst hits → that's C-Recovery territory. When breadth has been rising and a negative catalyst hits → that's A→B fade risk. The UMU narrative should explicitly call out whether a catalyst is hitting into exhausted or extended positioning.
