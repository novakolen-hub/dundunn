PLAYBOOK CARD: BIG DOG CONSOLIDATION TRADE (v1 — from transcript 1 of 6)
=========================================

Setup Name: Big-Dog-Wedge

WHAT IT IS:
Tight wedge/consolidation pattern in a stock in play. Price compresses into
increasingly narrow range (higher lows, lower highs) creating a coiled spring.
Build into position at bottom of wedge, scale aggressively on breakout with
volume confirmation. R:R can be 1:10 or better.

ENTRY CRITERIA (all required):
  1. Stock in Play — news catalyst (new product, earnings beat, drug data,
     guidance raise). Today's fundamentals meaningfully better than yesterday's.
  2. Relative Volume >= 3 — confirms institutional participation and liquidity.
  3. Tight Wedge Pattern — consecutive higher lows and lower highs forming
     compression. Tighter = bigger coil = bigger breakout.
  4. Time in Pattern >= 30 min — ideally 45-60+ minutes. Sub-15 minute
     "wedges" don't count. Longer = more shorts trapped, more longs committed.
  5. Preferably holding above VWAP — not mandatory (CYCC example was below
     VWAP on momentum stock) but strongly preferred.
  6. Daily chart context supports direction — breaking out to new highs or
     in uptrend. Don't fight the daily.
  7. SPY context aligned — market strength on your side or at minimum not
     actively selling off.

ENTRY EXECUTION (Tiered Scaling):
  - Tier 1: Buy at bottom of wedge when pattern forming. Small starter.
  - Tier 2: Add as time passes and pattern holds (30 min in, still tight = add).
  - Tier 3: Add more as it continues to hold (45-60 min).
  - Tier 4-5: Double up (or more) on breakout above wedge top WITH volume confirmation.
  - NO volume on breakout? Scale back to Tier 1 immediately.

STOP LOGIC:
  - Stop is below the bottom of the wedge pattern.
  - Key advantage: you know EXACTLY where you're wrong.
  - Price AND time matter: if you love the fundamentals, don't just puke on
    a wick below — watch if it closes/holds below.
  - Initial stop at bottom of wedge. Wider stop slightly below if conviction
    is very high.

TARGET LOGIC:
  - Capture the meat of the move, NOT bottom-to-top.
  - Use measured moves — if opening drive was 2 points, expect ~2 points from breakout.
  - Half off at first resistance / measured move. Trail the rest.
  - Reasons to sell: hits price target, breaks uptrend, big red candle with volume,
    gets below VWAP (if was holding above), two consecutive signals against you.
  - For swings: hold until you get a reason to sell (don't exit just because green).

IDEAL V9 CONDITIONS:
  - Gameplan A or B — needs market support. Momentum/breakout trade.
  - Session Phase: Best after open (post-price-discovery). 45-60 min consolidation
    after opening drive is sweet spot = MO to MM timing.
  - Works on both sides (long and short variants).

BOUNCE VARIANT (from TPX example):
  - Stock gaps down hard on bad news, sells off steeply
  - Goes sideways 1-2 hours (stops making new lows)
  - Wedge forms at the bottom
  - Breakout target = back to VWAP
  - Size smaller (Tier 1-3 max, NOT Tier 5)
  - Don't pick bottoms — let the wedge form first

WHEN NOT TO TAKE IT:
  - RVOL < 3 (not in play, no liquidity for size)
  - Wedge lasted < 15 minutes (not enough compression)
  - No catalyst / no fundamental change
  - Fighting the daily chart trend
  - Volume doesn't confirm breakout (scale back immediately)
  - GP-C or GP-D breadth (no institutional support) [NOTE: this rule
    is being revised — see Gameplan Rework section below]

BEHAVIORAL NOTES (from SMB):
  - Demands aggression. When you see it, trade it BIG.
  - Win rate may be ~50%. R:R (1:10 possible) makes it work over time.
  - Build a "book of charts" — screenshot every good wedge daily, review weekends.
  - Don't sloppy into entries — footprints get you worked out. Clean tiered entries.
  - The Big Dog trader back-tested variables in Excel on Saturdays. Mathematical
    edge > gut feel.

GAMEPLAN REWORK — IN PROGRESS
=========================================

PROBLEM:
GP-C currently = "Bench mode, sit out." But many trading days have flat breadth
with real opportunities. Example: March 2, 2026 — GP-C all day, gap down,
grinding up steadily. Return Pullback and VWAP reclaim setups were available
but system said sit out.

KEY INSIGHT:
Not all GP-C days are the same. There's a massive difference between:
  - C-Chop: Breadth bouncing +1/-1 with no direction. Genuinely nothing to do.
  - C-Recovery: Breadth was negative, grinding toward zero. Gap down being bought.
    Names setting up.
  - C-Compression: Breadth in tight range for hours. Coiling for a move.

PROPOSED FIX:
GP-C gets sub-modes based on trajectory and context:

  C + Trajectory Improving + Gap Down Recovered:
    → Allow: VWAP reclaim, Return Pullback, Big Dog bounce variant
    → Size: Tier 1-3 max (reduced from A/B levels)
    → In the game, but controlled

  C + Trajectory Flat + No Catalyst:
    → Genuine Bench. Nothing to do.

  C + Trajectory Fading from Positive:
    → Defensive only. Watch for short setups / Stuffed Trade.

Breadth score stays the same — what changes is the ACTION MAPPING.
Instead of binary "C = sit out," it becomes "C = reduced size + specific
setup types only, filtered by trajectory."

Some setups work fine on C days (VWAP Trade, Return Pullback, Big Dog bounce)
because they need structure and patience, not momentum. Momentum setups
(Gap and Go, Opening Drive, aggressive Breakout) stay locked out on GP-C.

DEPENDENCY:
Need all SMB playbook transcripts analyzed first to correctly map which setups
require what market conditions. Each playbook card will specify its minimum
gameplan/trajectory/RVOL requirements, then the matrix gets built from that data.

CRITICAL SYSTEM ISSUE: CONFLUENCE & GAMEPLAN LOCKOUT
=========================================
Date Added: March 2, 2026
Priority: HIGHEST — system is currently untradeable most days

PROBLEM STATEMENT:
Nothing on the Config watchlist ever reaches the 24/38 confluence
threshold needed to execute. The system designed to find opportunities
is filtering out ALL opportunities. Multiple layers compound to create
an impossibly high bar.

ROOT CAUSE ANALYSIS — 5 COMPOUNDING LAYERS:

Layer 1: Gameplan locks out live capital most days
  - GP-C range (-3 to +3 breadth) covers most normal market days
  - GP-C = "NO LIVE CAPITAL, SIM ONLY"
  - Result: majority of trading days are bench days

Layer 2: Confluence threshold is brutally high
  - Minimum to execute: 24/38 (63%)
  - On a GP-C day, Market Context category caps around 2/5
  - Remaining 5 categories must average 4.4/5 EACH plus positive modifiers
  - That's near-perfect alignment on everything simultaneously on a mediocre day

Layer 3: News/Catalyst modifier permanently stuck at 0
  - Range is +2 to -2 but NOT AUTOMATED (always 0)
  - Losing 2 potential points on top of Market Context cap
  - Makes reaching 24 nearly impossible on non-GP-A days

Layer 4: Watchlist is static
  - Only scores tickers on Config tab
  - Market generates new catalyst-driven opportunities daily
  - Stocks gapping 8% on earnings with RVOL 15 are invisible if not pre-loaded
  - SMB's entire approach = "stocks in play TODAY"
  - V9's current approach = "stocks I decided to watch YESTERDAY"

Layer 5: Universal scoring doesn't fit different setup types
  - One 38-point score tries to evaluate all setups identically
  - Big Dog Wedge needs: tight consolidation + volume + time (not momentum score)
  - Gap and Go needs: gap size + RVOL + catalyst (not key level proximity)
  - A stock can be perfect for a specific playbook but score 19/38 universally

COMPOUND EFFECT:
GP-C day + News/Catalyst always 0 + static watchlist + needing 24/38
= essentially requiring a perfect storm on a mediocre day
= system produces zero actionable trades most sessions

STRUCTURAL FIXES (Priority Order)
=========================================

FIX 1: GAMEPLAN C SUB-MODES (stop total lockout)
  Current: GP-C = NO LIVE CAPITAL
  Proposed: GP-C allows reduced size with trajectory context

  C-Recovery (breadth was negative, grinding positive, or gap down being bought):
    → Allow: VWAP reclaim, Return Pullback, Big Dog bounce, Second Day
    → Size: Tier 1-2 ($3K-$6K)
    → Threshold: setup-specific checklist (not universal 24/38)

  C-Compression (breadth tight range, coiling):
    → Allow: Big Dog Wedge, OR-Break (on breakout of range)
    → Size: Tier 1-2
    → Watch for breakout to upgrade to B in real-time

  C-Chop (breadth directionless, bouncing randomly):
    → Genuine bench. No live capital.
    → Sim only or study mode.

  C-Fade (breadth was positive, now fading toward zero):
    → Allow: short setups, Stuffed Trade, inverse ETFs
    → Size: Tier 1 only
    → Defensive posture

FIX 2: DYNAMIC TICKER SURFACING (let the market show you)
  New dashboard section: "In Play Today" or "Opportunity Scanner"
  Auto-populates when ANY ticker in the 424+ scanner universe meets:
    - Gap > 3% (either direction)
    - RVOL > 3
    - Has news catalyst (RSS hit within 24hrs)
    - Unusual volume spike intraday

  These are NOT watchlist tickers — they're the market telling you
  where the action is. Dashboard shows them as cards with full
  enrichment, setup detection, and playbook labels.

  Additional source: Arete morning call names. If Arete highlights
  a name, it should auto-surface on the dashboard even if not on Config.

  Scanner implementation:
    - Already polls 424+ tickers every cycle
    - Add filter pass: if gap > 3% OR rvol > 3 OR news match → flag
    - Include in getDashboardData() as "inPlayToday" array
    - Dashboard renders as new section above Watching

FIX 3: SETUP-SPECIFIC SCORING (replace universal confluence)
  Current: one 38-point scale for everything
  Proposed: each playbook has its own criteria checklist

  Example — Big Dog Wedge checklist:
    ☑ News catalyst today? (required)
    ☑ RVOL ≥ 3? (required)
    ☑ Tight wedge pattern ≥ 30 min? (required)
    ☑ Holding above VWAP? (preferred, not required)
    ☑ Daily chart supports direction? (required)
    ☑ SPY not actively selling? (required)
    ☑ Volume confirming breakout? (required for scaling up)
    → If 5/6 required criteria met = VALID SETUP, size per gameplan

  Example — Gap and Go checklist:
    ☑ Gap > 3%? (required)
    ☑ RVOL > 5? (required)
    ☑ Catalyst driving gap? (required)
    ☑ Holding above pre-market low? (required)
    ☑ First pullback held? (entry trigger)
    → If criteria met = VALID SETUP

  The universal confluence score can still exist as a secondary
  reference, but the GO/NO-GO decision comes from the playbook-
  specific checklist, not the universal number.

  This directly solves the "perfect for this setup but scores 19/38"
  problem.

FIX 4: AUTOMATE NEWS/CATALYST MODIFIER
  Scanner already pulls RSS news feeds per ticker.
  Logic:
    - If ticker has news hit today AND RVOL > 2 → Catalyst = +2
    - If ticker has news hit today AND RVOL 1-2 → Catalyst = +1
    - If no news → Catalyst = 0
    - If bad news + selling → Catalyst = -1 or -2

  Immediately adds 1-2 points to every name with a catalyst.
  Those are exactly the names you should be interested in.

  Implementation: score in scanner's enrichment pass, include
  in tickerQuotes data sent to dashboard.

FIX 5: THRESHOLD RECALIBRATION
  After fixes 1-4, revisit whether 24/38 is still the right number.
  May self-correct once:
    - Market Context isn't the only gatekeeper (setup-specific scoring)
    - Catalyst modifier adds real points
    - Dynamic tickers surface high-RVOL names naturally
    - GP-C allows controlled trading

  Possible new framework:
    - Setup-specific checklist = GO/NO-GO (binary)
    - Universal confluence = SIZING guidance
      28+ = full size per gameplan
      24-27 = standard size
      20-23 = reduced size (Tier 1-2)
      <20 = skip unless setup checklist is 100%
